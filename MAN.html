Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)

What is it?

A Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)is a network that connects devices across an entire town, city, or small region. It is larger than a local area network (LAN) but smaller than a wide area network (WAN). A WAN is used to connect devices over a large geographical area, such as across the country or continent. A MAN is located between a LAN and a WAN and can be used to connect multiple LANs. When one LAN uses modems, digital devices, and other media types to connect with another LAN, it covers a large area and is considered a MAN.


What does it do

A metropolitan area network (MAN) can connect various systems, individuals, and equipment through the Internet of Things. With the right network setup, if a traffic light malfunctions on the outskirts of the city, the relevant party can be quickly informed. A MAN also enables large entities, such as a city government or university, to consolidate resources and reduce inefficiencies in their budget, leading to cost-saving benefits:

  1. It reduces the number of licenses needed for various applications. Resources are shared
  2. You only need one firewall, streamlining your data security, keeping people safer.
  3. It minimizes data entry and data warehousing duplications. Information can up uploaded in one place, and everyone on the network has access
  4. It cuts down mistakes that happen when the left hand (Department A) doesn't know what the right hand (Department B) is doing
  5. The metropolitan area network speed can meet even the toughest data-transfer demands over a large area



How does it Work

Metropolitan area networks connect multiple LANs together through dedicated wired and wireless backbones. A MAN may use a local exchange carrier (LEC) to provide the connections between LANs and may connect to an internet exchange point for high-speed communication between the MAN and the public internet.
